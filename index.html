Introduction

Many students choose to listen to music while studying as a way to improve focus, reduce stress, and create a more engaging environment. According to Goltz and Sadakata (2021), students often use music intentionally as a cognitive tool, selecting specific genres to help regulate attention and productivity. However, the effectiveness of this strategy is not universal. While some learners report increased concentration, others experience distraction depending on the type of music and the task being performed. This raises an important question: does listening to music actually improve the ability to retain information, or does it interfere with learning?
